About Me

My research and work are at the intersection of data science, international policy, and decision analysis. I am particularly focused on how data-driven approaches can drive meaningful impact in sustainability, climate action, and social welfare.

Research Interests

I work extensively on developing and optimizing machine learning models that analyze environmental, social, and governance (ESG) data to derive meaningful insights. This involves collaborating with cross-functional teams to create AI-driven solutions that assist decision-makers in tackling sustainability challenges.

Decision Theory in International Politics

Building on my background in international and European politics, I have a keen interest in decision theory and how it impacts policy-making at a global level. This involves analyzing corporate power, global political economy, and creating tools to visualize the complex data surrounding these topics.
